# Queue-Times API — Additional Reference

## Data Freshness
Queue times are updated approximately every 5 minutes. The `last_updated` field on each ride is a UTC timestamp. If a ride shows `last_updated` more than 15 minutes ago, the park may have stopped sending data for that attraction (often because it's closed for the day or under refurbishment).

## Wait Time Interpretation
- `wait_time: 0` with `is_open: true` — ride is open with virtually no wait (walk-on).
- `wait_time: 0` with `is_open: false` — ride is closed (down, end of day, or seasonal).
- Very high wait times (90+ min) typically indicate a popular ride at peak hours or a limited-capacity experience.

## Park Groups
Parks are organized into groups (e.g., Disney, Universal, Six Flags). The `list_parks` tool returns the full hierarchy. Each park group has an `id`, `name`, and a `parks` array. Individual park `id` values are what `get_queue_times` expects.

## Lands vs Rides
`get_queue_times` returns two keys:
- `lands` — rides grouped by themed area (useful for giving area-based advice).
- `rides` — a flat list of all rides in the park (useful for sorting by wait time).
Both contain the same ride data; `lands` just provides extra grouping context.

## Coverage
Not all parks worldwide are covered. Availability is stronger for major US and European parks. If a user asks about a park not in `list_parks`, let them know it may not be supported yet.
